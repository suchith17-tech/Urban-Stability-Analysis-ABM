## Phase 2 — Monthly Resource Supply System (This Session)

### Step 4: Water Supply (`04_process_water.py`)
- **Input**: `water supply data.xlsx`
- **Output**: `water_supply_monthly.csv` (60 rows: 5 cities × 12 months)
- **Features**:
  - City-specific seasonal multipliers (not one-size-fits-all):
    - Belagavi: summer 55%, monsoon 130% (rain-dependent, Malaprabha catchment)
    - Bengaluru: summer 70%, monsoon 115% (Cauvery + groundwater, moderate)
    - Mangaluru: summer 85%, monsoon 135% (Netravathi, coastal stable)
    - Hubballi-Dharwad: summer 55%, monsoon 110% (semi-arid interior, severe)
    - Mysuru: summer 75%, monsoon 112% (KRS reservoir, moderate)
  - LPCD 135 compliance check per month
  - `water_per_hh_lpd` column: per-household litres/day for ABM allocation
- **Verified**: Bengaluru Jan = 616 L/HH/day ✅ (matches 135 LPCD × 4.6 HH size)

### Step 5: Electricity Supply (`05_process_electricity.py`)
- **Input**: `electricity consumption data.xlsx`, `karnataka_electricity_timeseries.csv`
- **Output**: `electricity_supply_monthly.csv` (60 rows)
- **Features**:
  - Base from city-level Annual MU snapshots
  - Growth rate from state-level time-series (2010-2023): 5.8% annual
  - Monthly demand multipliers: summer peak +10%, monsoon +5%, post-monsoon -10%
  - **Stochastic noise**: ±5% normal months, ±10% peak months (seeded RNG)
  - `electricity_per_hh_kwh` column + Gruha Jyothi 200 kWh threshold
  - `shock_multiplier_base` column for ABM conditional shocks
- **Verified**: Bengaluru Jan = 564 kWh/HH/month ✅

### Step 6: Food Ration Supply (`06_process_food_ration.py`)
- **Input**: `feb -apr 2025 (Rice/Wheat/Dal).xlsx`
- **Output**: `food_ration_supply.csv` (60 rows)
- **Critical Fixes Applied**:
  1. **Column parsing**: col[9] = "Quantity Involved (Quintals)" is the actual food quantity, NOT col[1] (shops) or col[5] (RC members). 1 Quintal = 100 KG.
  2. **Core-taluk-only mapping**: Only city taluks mapped (not entire district). Previous version mapped 14 Belagavi district taluks to the city, inflating food 10×.
  3. **Wheat/Dal synthesis**: Those files had 0 in quantity column → synthesised from PDS entitlement norms (AAY: 10kg wheat + 5kg dal, BPL: 7+3, APL: 2+1)
  4. **Per-HH denominator**: Uses `min(total_rc, households)` since PDS is for ration card holders only. Added `pds_coverage_ratio` column.
  5. **Stochastic noise**: ±10% per commodity per month (supply chain variability)
  6. **Shock columns**: `drought_shock_multiplier`, `flood_shock_multiplier` (default 1.0)
- **Verified**: Bengaluru = 30 kg/HH/month (PDS only — correct), Mysuru = 59 kg ✅

## Phase 3 — Demand Calibration & Resource Interactions (This Session)

### Step 7: Demand Calibration (`07_process_hces.py`)
- **Input**: `consumer details per capita.xlsx` (MPCE by district × expenditure sub-group)
- **Output**: `demand_calibration.csv` (15 rows: 5 cities × 3 groups)
- **Method**:
  - District MPCE → scale by group ratio (extreme_poor: 0.38×, bpl_poor: 0.72×, non_poor: 1.85×)
  - ₹ → physical units via Karnataka tariff rates:
    - Food: ₹15/kg (poor PDS blend) to ₹45/kg (rich market basket)
    - Electricity: ₹4.5–6.5/kWh (BESCOM slab average by group)
    - Water: Housing MPCE × 5% water fraction ÷ ₹8/kL
  - **PDS share decomposition**: extreme_poor 60% PDS, bpl_poor 35%, non_poor 10%
  - Added `pds_food_kg_hh` and `market_food_kg_hh` columns
- **Verified**: Bengaluru BPL PDS need 38 kg vs PDS supply 30 kg → ratio 0.79 (realistic near-adequacy)

### Step 8: Resource Interaction Matrix (`08_resource_interactions.py`)
- **Input**: `consumer details per capita.xlsx` (all 27 Karnataka districts)
- **Output**: `resource_interaction_matrix.json`
- **Coupling Coefficients**:

## Key Decisions & Rationale

| Decision | Rationale |
|---|---|
| Monthly timestep | Aligns water (seasonal), electricity (growth), and food (ration cycle) on same time axis |
| City-specific water multipliers | Belagavi (rain-fed) has different hydrology than Mangaluru (coastal) |
| Stochastic noise in supply | "Seasonal + stochastic + heterogeneous" — real systems have variability |
| PDS as partial food supply | PDS covers 10-60% of food needs; market supplies the rest |
| Correlation-based interactions | Data-grounded proxy for causal coupling; academically disclosed |
| Core-taluk-only food mapping | District taluks inflate city food by 10× — only urban taluks valid |
